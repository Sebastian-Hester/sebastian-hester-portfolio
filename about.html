Enhanced Biography and Career Goals Essay

Sebastian Hester

← Back to Portfolio

My name is Sebastian Hester, and I am a student at the University of South Florida pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Information Science with a concentration in Information Security. I am originally from St. Petersburg Florida, but currently I live in Wesley Chapel Florida. Growing up, I was always fascinated by technology and how it impacts our everyday lives. Whether it was learning how computers worked, troubleshooting devices for friends and family, or exploring new software, I found myself naturally drawn to technology and problem-solving. Over time, that interest developed into a passion for information systems, data analysis, software development, and cybersecurity.

My experience at USF has given me the opportunity to explore many different areas within the information science field. When I first began the program, I knew I enjoyed technology, but I wasn't entirely sure which path I wanted to pursue. Through my coursework, I discovered interests in data analytics, software development, data visualization, and information security. One of the things I enjoy most about the field is that technology is constantly evolving, which means there is always something new to learn and new challenges to solve.

Throughout the BSIS program, I have taken courses covering a wide range of topics including data analysis, database management, information security, networking, systems analysis, programming, and information policy. These courses have helped me develop both technical and analytical skills while also teaching me how technology supports businesses and organizations. I have gained experience working with SQL databases, writing programs in Java and Python, analyzing data, and understanding how security principles help protect information systems. More importantly, I have learned how to approach problems critically and think about technology solutions from both a technical and business perspective.

One of my favorite experiences during my time at USF was developing a Secure Reservation API as part of my coursework and personal development. This project allowed me to combine several areas that interest me, including software development, database management, analytics, and security. Using Python, FastAPI, SQLAlchemy, and SQLite, I built a reservation system that included user authentication, password hashing, reservation management, and data reporting features. I also used Tableau to create dashboards and visualizations that transformed reservation data into meaningful business insights. Working on this project helped me realize how much I enjoy building systems that not only function well but also provide valuable information through data analysis. It was one of the first projects where I truly felt like I was applying everything I had learned throughout my degree program into one complete solution.

Outside of the classroom, I have spent several years working at Starbucks. While it may not be a traditional technology role, it has taught me many skills that I believe are just as important as technical knowledge. Working in a fast-paced environment has strengthened my communication, teamwork, leadership, customer service, and time management skills. It has taught me how to adapt quickly, solve problems under pressure, and work effectively with people from different backgrounds. These experiences have helped me become a more well-rounded professional and have shown me the value of balancing technical skills with strong interpersonal skills.

As I prepare to graduate, I am excited about the opportunities ahead and the different directions my career could take. One of my biggest career goals is to work for Walt Disney World in a technology-focused role. Disney has always stood out to me because of its commitment to innovation, guest experience, and operational excellence. I am especially interested in roles involving logistics and analytics, software engineering, business intelligence, or information technology. I am fascinated by how large organizations use technology and data to improve operations and make strategic decisions, and I would love to contribute to that process at Disney.

At the same time, I am also interested in opportunities within the financial sector, particularly in analytics and technology roles with organizations such as Citi, DTCC, or similar companies. Financial institutions rely heavily on technology, data analysis, and secure information systems, making them exciting environments for someone with my background and interests. Whether I find myself working in entertainment, finance, healthcare, or another industry, my goal is to build a career where I can use technology and data to solve meaningful problems and create value for organizations.

Looking ahead, I plan to continue learning long after graduation. Technology changes rapidly, and I believe continuous learning is one of the most important qualities an information professional can have. In the near future, I would like to pursue certifications such as CompTIA Security+ to strengthen my cybersecurity knowledge and expand my professional credentials. I also plan to continue developing my programming skills, learning new technologies, and gaining additional experience in data analytics and business intelligence tools. Industry resources such as ONET OnLine emphasize the growing importance of technical skills, problem-solving abilities, and continuous professional development in technology-related careers (National Center for O*NET Development, 2024).

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (2019), careers in software development are projected to grow much faster than the average for all occupations over the next decade. Similarly, careers involving data analytics and operations research are expected to remain in high demand as organizations increasingly rely on data-driven decision-making (U.S. BUREAU OF LABOR STATISTICS, 2025) This growth is encouraging because it reflects the increasing importance of technology and data across virtually every industry. As organizations continue to rely on information systems to support operations and decision-making, professionals who can bridge the gap between technology and business needs will remain in demand.

Looking back on my time at USF, I am proud of how much I have grown both academically and personally. The BSIS program has challenged me to develop technical skills, strengthen my analytical thinking, and become a better communicator and problem solver. It has also helped me gain confidence in my abilities and given me a clearer vision for my future career. While graduation marks the end of one chapter, I see it as the beginning of another. I am excited to continue learning, growing, and pursuing opportunities that allow me to combine my interests in technology, analytics, software development, and information security. Most importantly, I look forward to building a career that allows me to make a meaningful impact while continuing to do work that I genuinely enjoy.
